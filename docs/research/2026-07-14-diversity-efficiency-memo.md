# Diversity efficiency: the finding, its wound, and the program

Status: active investigation. One claim witnessed, one preregistered and
awaiting its judge, the headline interpretation contested by our own
adversarial review before publication. All numbers from n=110 paired-arm
bench artifacts, content-addressed in this repo.

## What is measured and stands

- **The 3b uplift is real.** 9/110 bare to 21/110 wrapped at best-of-3: 12
  rescues, 0 regressions; the Newcombe interval excludes zero and the exact
  paired test is stronger still.
- **Capability-per-GB peaks mid-ladder.** Wrapped pass per B-param: 0.036
  (0.5b), 0.064 (3b), 0.036 (7b). The honest claim is a SWEET-SPOT claim:
  on this machine, a mid-size model under the verified loop is the optimum,
  not "smaller is better."
- **eta ordering as arithmetic**: 0.507 / 0.757 / 0.269 (verified
  independently). Its interpretation is the contested part.

## The wound, self-inflicted on purpose

Our adversarial math review found the naive reading unsafe:

1. **Jensen bias**: the independence prediction 1-(1-p̄)^3 uses the
   aggregate rate; concavity means task heterogeneity alone deflates
   measured eta, more for models with more bimodal task mastery — the 7b's
   low eta can be produced with zero diversity change.
2. **Wrong null**: candidate 1 is the greedy attempt itself, so the true
   pipeline is greedy + best-of-2 at T=0.8; the correct per-task null is
   1-(1-b_i)(1-q_i)^2, and eta conflates the temperature rate-shift with
   diversity.
3. **eta(0.5b) rests on one rescued task** — statistically vacuous.
4. The preregistered band tests eta-constancy in N for one model, not the
   cross-scale claim (frozen in the claims addendum before the result).

## Prior art (live-fetched; the direction is real, the metric may be new)

- Smaller BASE models are measurably more diverse: entropy falls
  monotonically with scale (REAL Sampling, arXiv 2406.07735); Qwen3-base
  pass@k crossovers with strictly monotonic diversity measures (S2L-PO,
  arXiv 2605.30789).
- Weak model + many samples + verifier beats strong model per cost (Large
  Language Monkeys, arXiv 2407.21787); small models Pareto-optimal at
  matched inference FLOPs (arXiv 2408.00724, 2408.03314).
- The aggregate-independence bias is known and modeled (Schaeffer,
  arXiv 2502.17578; beta-binomial pass@k, arXiv 2510.05197) — as fitting
  corrections, not as a scalar efficiency compared across scales.
- Diversity collapse is firmly established for ALIGNMENT (RLHF/RLVR:
  arXiv 2310.06452, 2504.13837, 2406.05587), less settled for raw scale.
- **No prior work found that names gain-over-independence as a scalar
  diversity efficiency across scales** (moderate confidence: absence after
  targeted search, not proof). The corrected, per-task version of eta may
  be a real contribution if the program below confirms it measures what it
  claims.

## The program (preregistered protocol, ~20 model-hours)

E0 determinism gate → E1 iid candidate panels per task (the discriminating
data; per-task p_i kill the Jensen confound) → E2 synthetic reconstruction
with the correct greedy+T0.8 null → E3 wrapper-prompt ablation → E4
mutated-twin lane (memorization control) → E5 the sealed best-of-5
adjudication (running now) → E6 temperature probe. Overall verdict rule
frozen: the hypothesis graduates only if the corrected cross-scale
contrast, its artifact-level mechanism corroboration, the contamination
check, and the preregistration all hold together — anything else ships
with honest nulls, no narrative rescue. The bench now records per-task
outcome vectors on every run, so E1's unit of analysis is native.

## Why this is the platform's proof of concept

The candidate discovery was generated by our own receipts (the frontier
table), formalized as a preregistered claim, wounded by our own adversary
before anyone else could, positioned against live literature, and given a
falsifiable program — all within hours, all on local hardware, all
re-runnable by a stranger. Whether eta survives, the method already did.
