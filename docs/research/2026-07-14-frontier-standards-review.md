This is the field's most prominent live proposal for institutionalized
frontier evaluation, written by someone whose sincerity this review has
no reason to doubt and does not question. That is precisely why it is
the right test case for the discipline: the robe test is a test of
architecture, not of character. The three properties that make a robe:
the judge authors the criterion, the judge benefits from the verdict,
and the verdict cannot be re-checked. Scored clause by clause.

## Where the proposal genuinely inverts the robe

- **"Open to any organisation by building models that meet the
  benchmark criteria."** The oracle keeps no guest list. This is the
  first inversion stated plainly, and it is the proposal's best
  sentence.
- **Held-out tests "independent of the Labs to prevent overfitting."**
  Criterion authorship moved outside the judged. Goodhart is named as
  a design constraint rather than discovered as a scandal. Credit.
- **"An ecosystem of third-party auditors."** The third inversion in
  embryo: someone other than the instrument's owner probes the
  instrument.
- **Quarterly deprecation of saturated benchmarks.** Correct
  instinct: an eval is a dated instrument, not a monument.

## Where the robe re-enters

- **The judged fund the judge.** "Funding would need to be substantial
  and likely mostly come from industry." The FINRA precedent cuts both
  ways: FINRA is funded and substantially governed by the member firms
  it regulates, and the capture critiques of that arrangement are a
  documented literature (moderate confidence on the critique details,
  high on the funding structure). A body whose payroll depends on the
  entities it grades holds a conflicted pen.
- **The body mints standing.** "Being designated a Frontier Lab would
  carry significant prestige." Standing is exactly what the robe
  controls. A designation that confers prestige creates a constituency
  for lenient thresholds on both sides of the table, and the proposal
  offers no mechanism that lets an outsider re-derive why a
  designation was granted or refused.
- **No audit of the auditor.** The body develops assessment protocols,
  determines thresholds, and updates them. Nowhere does the proposal
  subject the body's own instruments to adversarial audit or publish
  their failure record. The measured base rates say this is not a
  hypothetical worry: a flagship benchmark needed a third to two
  thirds of raw tasks flagged to be usable, and false-pass patches sat
  behind a quarter of a leaderboard's entries. A Standards Body
  running unaudited closed evals is a bishop with a test suite,
  however sincere.
- **"If deemed necessary."** The slowdown clause vests the largest
  possible verdict in an undefined "deemed," with no named criterion,
  no receipt, and no appeal path. This may be unavoidable for genuine
  emergencies; it should at minimum be pre-registered: what evidence,
  measured how, triggers what.
- **Rotation without pinning destroys comparability.** Quarterly
  benchmark replacement is right, but unless every verdict pins its
  benchmark version and hash, "Frontier-class in Q1" and
  "Frontier-class in Q3" are incommensurable claims wearing one label.
  Version-pinned lanes are a solved problem at small scale; nothing
  prevents them at large scale except the habit of not keeping
  receipts.

## The hard case, honestly

Dual-use evaluation content (bio, cyber) cannot carry public receipts,
and this review does not pretend it can. But re-checkability does not
require publicity. It requires layered verification: public hashes of
sealed protocols, cleared third parties who can re-walk the full
chain, and published adjudication statistics whose inputs stay closed.
The choice is not between secrecy and receipts; it is between secrecy
with receipts and secrecy with trust-me.

## Scorecard

Roughly one and a half of three inversions. The proposal moves
criterion authorship partly outside the judged and gestures at
third-party audit, while leaving the body's own instruments unaudited,
its funding conflicted, its designations unreceipted, and its gravest
power undefined. It is a better bishop, partially instrumented. The
missing half is not politics; it is evaluation engineering, and every
missing piece has a working small-scale existence proof in this
repository: oracle-strength audits published whichever way they fall,
sealed thresholds adjudicated without rescue, version-pinned verdicts,
and an instrument register that reports its own rot.

## Sincerity and the thread

The operator's formulation, same day: sincerity is the thread that
lifts the robe; sincerity outranks authority. The review's refinement:
sincerity is the necessary thread and cannot be the whole garment,
because sincerity is a property of the speaker that no outsider can
inspect. Gibran's parishioners believed their bishop sincere. What
lifts the robe is sincerity that submits to instruments: conviction
willing to pin its criteria, hash its verdicts, and publish its
misses. A receipt is sincerity externalized, checkable by a stranger
who owes the speaker nothing. The proposal reviewed here reads as
sincere. The architecture it sketches does not yet let anyone verify
that the institution it births will stay that way, and institutions
outlive their founders' sincerity. That is what the receipts are for.
