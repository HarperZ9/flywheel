Four rooms: institutional consensus, insider forecast, running
commentary, civilizational critique. One shared architecture: in every
room the claim's weight is carried by WHO stands behind it (a signatory
roster, insider status, a monitoring posture, an endorsement list) and
in no room does a mechanism exist to score the claims when their dates
arrive. The discourse has authority in abundance and adjudication
nowhere.

## The honorable partial exception, half-elapsed

Aschenbrenner gave dates and numbers, which makes him the only
falsifiable author in the set, and two years have now passed. The
result is instructive: his 2025/26 claim ("AI outperforms college
graduates") is DUE, and it cannot be cleanly adjudicated because no
metric, population, or task distribution was sealed at publication.
Frontier models exceed graduates on many exam-style benchmarks while
labor-substitution evidence stays mixed, so believers and skeptics both
claim the verdict. An unpreregistered prediction does not become false;
it becomes an argument, permanently. That is the precise failure the
sealed-claims machinery here exists to prevent, demonstrated at
civilization scale by the field's most-read forecast.

## The convergent simile

The Stanford statement and the Hassabis post independently reach for
the same comparison: larger than the Industrial Revolution, over a
much shorter time. Neither attaches a quantity, baseline, or
measurement. When the two most institutionally weighty artifacts in
the discourse converge on the same unquantified rhetoric, that is
evidence the discourse lacks instruments, not evidence about the
future.

## Poured back

`dataset/ai_forecast_claims.jsonl`: the set's adjudicable and
pseudo-adjudicable claims as dated records with frozen source hashes,
due dates, and honest readout status, extending the physics
stale-claims lane to the AI discourse itself. Three of five records
carry the readout "UNSEALED BY SOURCE", which is the dataset's real
finding: the register can hold these claims but cannot score them,
because their authors kept the criteria unwritten. The register that
would fix this is the machinery this repository already runs on its
own claims: seal the readout with the prediction, adjudicate on the
date, no rescue. The k=5 forecast preregistered through crucible this
morning is the working counter-example, at small scale, of what the
discourse could do at large scale.

## Boundary

Nothing here scores the four sources' worldviews. Alarm, consensus,
monitoring, and refusal are all positions a person may sincerely hold;
this note measures only whether each room's claims can be checked by a
stranger, and records that in every room the answer is not yet.
