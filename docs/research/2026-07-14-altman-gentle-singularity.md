## Read against the loop (honestly, not dismissively)

These are consequential claims from someone with the capability to move
toward them, and the loop's job is not to sneer but to ask the credo's one
question: where is the receipt a stranger can re-run? By tenet 4 (no claim
without its interval), the dated predictions are unanchored: "novel
insights by 2026," "abundant by the 2030s" carry no falsification
condition, no measured interval, no error bar. That does not make them
wrong; it makes them, in our language, UNVERIFIABLE until a check that can
fail is named. The honest posture is to hold them as hypotheses with a
timestamp, not evidence.

The sharpest tension is methodological, and worth stating plainly.
"Iterative deployment lets society adapt" places the WORLD's after-the-fact
adaptation where the credo places an EXTERNAL CHECK before acceptance.
Ship-then-observe is a different acceptance rule from verify-then-ship;
both are defensible, but they are not the same, and the loop's entire
existence is the wager that the second is more accountable at high stakes.
The recursive-self-improvement framing connects directly to the weco AIDE2
finding this session: their measured version cut reward-hacking 63% to 34%
and explicitly did NOT ignite. "Passed the event horizon" is the ungated
version of the same claim AIDE2 measured and bounded. The measured one is
the more honest artifact.

Where it touches the mission: nothing here changes a claim of ours, but it
sharpens the WHY. A world of cheap, abundant intelligence raises the value
of the one thing cheap intelligence does not supply, a check the model did
not author. If Altman is right about abundance, the scarce good becomes
the receipt. That is the market the flywheel is built for, and it is worth
saying without triumph: the more capable the proposer, the more the
accountable disposer matters.

No claim of ours changes on this source alone.
