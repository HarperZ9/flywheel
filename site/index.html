the seam

The small tear we are closing

The frontier is remarkable, and the strongest reasoning today lives in hosted models. We are not here to out-reason them. We are here to make the model in front of you, whichever one it is, more efficient and more accountable to you.

Availability you control

Frontier models come and go on a schedule set by export policy and approval lists. A model on weights you hold is here when you need it, on an airplane, when the network is down.

A record you can keep

Every accepted answer carries a receipt: the inputs, the check that passed, and hashes anyone can re-run offline. You never have to take it on faith.

Efficiency, not replacement

Flywheel can sit underneath any harness, cache what it has already verified, and route only the genuinely hard part of a task to the expensive tier.

Smaller and smaller hardware

A capable coder in a single file just under 9 GB runs on an ordinary machine today. The work ahead is keeping more capability in less space.

the whole application

Ten tools, one spine

Each of these is a real recording, captured by a zero-dependency recorder that runs the tool and writes a self-contained player plus a receipt with a hash over every step. Open any one. It plays offline, no network, no dependencies.

honest measurement

What we can prove today, and what we cannot yet

The model is Flywheel-Local-Coder-14B, a trained artifact with a full provenance chain. We hold ourselves to what an outside observer can check. Here is the first hard-set run, with confidence intervals, not adjectives.

ArmHard set (10 tasks)Wilson 95% CIReceipts
single-shot8 / 10 (80%)[0.490, 0.943]100%
verified inference9 / 10 (90%)[0.596, 0.982]100%
best-of-49 / 10 (90%)[0.596, 0.982]100%
single + oracle8 / 10 (80%)[0.490, 0.943]100%
We do not claim a capability uplift. Verified inference beats single-shot by +0.100 here, but the 95% interval is [-0.236, +0.420], which includes zero, and plain best-of-4 sampling ties it. So the honest, measurable claims today are 100% receipt reproducibility, pass parity, and local cost. A real effect at this size would need roughly a hundred tasks to separate from noise, which is exactly what the curated hard lane is building toward. The number moves the day the evidence does, not before.

Run it on your own machine

One file, just under 9 GB. A GPU helps but is not required. Your prompts and your code never leave your disk.

ollama create flywheel-local-coder-14b -f Modelfile
ollama run flywheel-local-coder-14b
